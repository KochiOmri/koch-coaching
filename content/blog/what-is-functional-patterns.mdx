## What is Functional Patterns?

Functional Patterns (FP) is a biomechanics-based training system that focuses on how the human body was designed to move. Founded by **Naudi Aguilar**, FP takes a radically different approach from conventional fitness and physical therapy by prioritizing movement quality over everything else.

Instead of isolating muscles or chasing arbitrary fitness metrics, Functional Patterns trains the body through the lens of human evolution — focusing on the movements that shaped our anatomy over millions of years.

## The Big 4: Movements That Define Us

At the core of Functional Patterns are the **Big 4** — the four fundamental movement patterns that every human body is built to perform:

### 1. Standing

Standing may seem passive, but proper upright posture requires a complex interplay of muscles, fascia, and joint alignment. FP addresses how your body organizes itself against gravity, ensuring that you can stand efficiently without compensations that lead to pain.

### 2. Walking (Gait)

Walking is the most fundamental human movement. FP places enormous emphasis on gait mechanics — the way your arms swing, your hips rotate, and your feet strike the ground. Dysfunctional gait patterns are often the root cause of chronic pain that people spend years trying to fix.

### 3. Running

Running amplifies every pattern present in walking. It demands greater force production, more elastic recoil from connective tissue, and precise timing of muscle activation. FP trains the body to handle these demands without breaking down.

### 4. Throwing

The throwing pattern encompasses any rotational movement — from literally throwing a ball to swinging a racket or even pushing a door. This cross-body rotation is what separates humans from other primates and is critical for upper body health.

## Why Functional Patterns is Different

Most training systems focus on **what** muscles do (flexion, extension, abduction). Functional Patterns focuses on **how** the body integrates movement as a whole system. Key differences include:

- **Root cause focus**: Rather than treating symptoms, FP identifies the biomechanical dysfunctions causing your pain
- **Gravity-based training**: Every exercise respects how gravity acts on the body in real-world positions
- **Myofascial integration**: Muscles don't work in isolation — FP trains the fascial chains that connect your whole body
- **Progressive complexity**: Training builds from corrective work to dynamic, athletic movement

## Who is Functional Patterns For?

FP is for anyone who wants to move better and live without pain. Whether you're dealing with chronic back pain, recovering from an injury, or looking to optimize athletic performance, the methodology meets you where you are.

Common reasons people seek out FP coaching:

- Chronic pain that hasn't responded to traditional treatments
- Poor posture from desk work or sedentary lifestyles
- Athletic performance plateaus
- Post-surgical rehabilitation
- Prevention and longevity
